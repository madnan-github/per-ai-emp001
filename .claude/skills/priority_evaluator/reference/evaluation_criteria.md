# Priority Evaluation Criteria

## Core Evaluation Dimensions

### Impact Assessment
- **Business Impact Score**: Measure of potential positive impact on business objectives
  - Revenue generation potential
  - Cost reduction capability
  - Market position improvement
  - Customer satisfaction enhancement
  - Operational efficiency gains

- **User Impact Score**: Measure of effect on end-user experience
  - Pain point resolution
  - Feature demand intensity
  - Usability improvements
  - Accessibility enhancements
  - Performance gains

- **Strategic Alignment Score**: How well the item aligns with organizational goals
  - Vision and mission alignment
  - Long-term strategic objectives
  - Competitive positioning
  - Innovation advancement
  - Brand reputation enhancement

### Effort Assessment
- **Implementation Complexity**: Technical difficulty of execution
  - Development effort required
  - Design complexity
  - Integration challenges
  - Testing requirements
  - Deployment complexity

- **Resource Requirements**: People, time, and budget needed
  - Personnel skills and availability
  - Timeline estimation
  - Budget allocation
  - Technology infrastructure
  - Third-party dependencies

- **Risk Assessment**: Potential negative consequences
  - Technical risks
  - Business risks
  - Security vulnerabilities
  - Compliance issues
  - Operational disruption

### Urgency Assessment
- **Time Sensitivity**: How time-critical is the item
  - Regulatory deadlines
  - Market timing windows
  - Seasonal opportunities
  - Competitive threats
  - Customer commitments

- **Dependency Impact**: Effect on other work
  - Prerequisites for other tasks
  - Blocker removal
  - Parallel work enablement
  - Resource unlocking
  - Knowledge transfer needs

- **Window of Opportunity**: How long the opportunity exists
  - Market window duration
  - Competitive window
  - Budget cycle timing
  - Resource availability window
  - Technology sunset dates

## Scoring Methodologies

### Weighted Scoring Model
- **Impact Weight**: 50% (Business Impact 25%, User Impact 15%, Strategic Alignment 10%)
- **Effort Weight**: 30% (Inverse - lower effort = higher score)
- **Urgency Weight**: 20% (Time Sensitivity 10%, Dependency Impact 5%, Window of Opportunity 5%)

### Scoring Scale
- **Critical (5)**: Must be done immediately, significant negative impact if not done
- **High (4)**: Should be done soon, substantial positive/negative impact
- **Medium (3)**: Would be beneficial, moderate impact
- **Low (2)**: Nice to have, minor impact
- **Minimal (1)**: Little to no impact, low priority

## Contextual Adjustments

### Situational Factors
- **Company Stage Adjustment**: Weight differently based on company maturity
  - Startup: Higher weight on market opportunities
  - Growth: Higher weight on scalability
  - Mature: Higher weight on optimization
  - Turnaround: Higher weight on critical fixes

- **Resource Constraints**: Adjust priorities based on available resources
  - Limited budget: Higher weight on low-effort, high-impact items
  - Limited personnel: Higher weight on parallelizable tasks
  - Tight timeline: Higher weight on quick wins
  - Abundant resources: Higher weight on strategic investments

- **Risk Tolerance**: Adjust based on organizational risk appetite
  - Conservative: Higher weight on proven, low-risk items
  - Moderate: Balanced approach to risk and reward
  - Aggressive: Higher weight on innovative, high-risk opportunities

### Stakeholder Influence
- **Decision Maker Priority**: Weight based on leadership priorities
- **Customer Urgency**: Weight based on customer demands
- **Regulatory Requirements**: Mandatory compliance items
- **Partner Dependencies**: Items affecting key partnerships
- **Team Capacity**: Alignment with team strengths and availability

## Evaluation Process

### Initial Screening
- **Minimum Viable Criteria**: Basic requirements to qualify
  - Clear definition and scope
  - Defined success metrics
  - Identified stakeholders
  - Available resources
  - Reasonable timeline

- **Disqualifying Factors**: Automatic rejection criteria
  - Contradicts company strategy
  - Violates compliance requirements
  - Impossible with current technology
  - Ethical concerns
  - Legal restrictions

### Detailed Evaluation
- **Multi-Source Input**: Gather input from various perspectives
  - Subject matter experts
  - End users
  - Stakeholders
  - Leadership
  - Cross-functional teams

- **Quantitative Measures**: Use data-driven metrics where possible
  - Historical performance data
  - Market research
  - Customer feedback metrics
  - Financial projections
  - Technical benchmarks

- **Qualitative Assessment**: Consider non-quantifiable factors
  - Strategic intuition
  - Market positioning
  - Cultural fit
  - Innovation potential
  - Learning opportunities

### Review and Validation
- **Peer Review**: Cross-check with other evaluators
- **Stakeholder Validation**: Confirm with affected parties
- **Leadership Alignment**: Ensure executive buy-in
- **Reality Check**: Validate against resource constraints
- **Risk Assessment**: Final risk evaluation

## Scoring Adjustments

### Positive Adjustments
- **Synergistic Effects**: Creates value beyond the immediate item
- **Platform Effects**: Enables future work or capabilities
- **Network Effects**: Becomes more valuable with adoption
- **Learning Value**: Provides knowledge for future projects
- **Reputation Benefits**: Enhances brand or credibility

### Negative Adjustments
- **Technical Debt**: Creates future maintenance burden
- **Complexity Penalty**: Increases overall system complexity
- **Maintenance Burden**: Ongoing support requirements
- **Security Concerns**: Introduces vulnerabilities
- **Scalability Issues**: Limits future growth options

## Decision Framework

### Priority Tiers
- **Tier 1 (Immediate)**: Start immediately, stop other work if necessary
  - Critical security issues
  - Compliance violations
  - Revenue-impacting bugs
  - Legal requirements
  - Core functionality failures

- **Tier 2 (This Quarter)**: Planned for immediate execution
  - High-value customer requests
  - Strategic initiatives
  - Competitive responses
  - High ROI opportunities
  - Prerequisite work

- **Tier 3 (This Half)**: Scheduled for mid-term execution
  - Valuable feature additions
  - Process improvements
  - Market expansion
  - Technical debt reduction
  - Infrastructure upgrades

- **Tier 4 (Future)**: Planned for later consideration
  - Nice-to-have features
  - Experimental projects
  - Long-term strategic bets
  - Low-priority requests
  - Research initiatives

### Trade-off Considerations
- **Speed vs. Quality**: Balance quick delivery with excellence
- **Individual vs. Group**: Optimize for individuals vs. teams
- **Short-term vs. Long-term**: Immediate gains vs. future benefits
- **Innovation vs. Stability**: New features vs. reliability
- **Growth vs. Profitability**: Market share vs. revenue focus
