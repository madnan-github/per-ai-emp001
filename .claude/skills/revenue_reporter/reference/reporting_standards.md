# Revenue Reporting Standards

## General Reporting Principles

### Accuracy Standards
- **Data Integrity**: All reported revenue figures must be reconciled with accounting systems
- **Verification**: Revenue data must be validated against supporting documentation
- **Audit Trail**: Maintain complete audit trail from transaction to report
- **Reconciliation**: Monthly reconciliation with financial records required

### Timeliness Requirements
- **Monthly Reports**: Due within 5 business days of month-end
- **Quarterly Reports**: Due within 10 business days of quarter-end
- **Annual Reports**: Due within 15 business days of year-end
- **Ad-hoc Reports**: Due within 24-48 hours depending on complexity

### Consistency Standards
- **Methodology**: Use consistent calculation methods across reporting periods
- **Definitions**: Apply uniform definitions for all metrics
- **Periods**: Maintain consistent reporting periods
- **Presentation**: Use consistent format and structure

## Revenue Recognition Standards

### ASC 606 Compliance
- **Identify Contract**: Properly identify all revenue contracts
- **Performance Obligations**: Identify distinct performance obligations
- **Transaction Price**: Determine total transaction price
- **Allocation**: Allocate price to performance obligations
- **Recognition**: Recognize revenue when obligations satisfied

### Timing Requirements
- **Point in Time**: Recognize revenue at point of transfer for goods
- **Over Time**: Recognize revenue over time for services
- **Control Transfer**: Recognize when control transfers to customer
- **Contract Modifications**: Properly account for contract changes

### Documentation Requirements
- **Contracts**: Maintain copies of all revenue contracts
- **Performance Obligations**: Document distinct performance obligations
- **Allocations**: Record allocation of transaction prices
- **Modifications**: Document contract modifications and impacts

## Data Sources and Validation

### Primary Data Sources
- **ERP System**: SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, etc.
- **CRM System**: Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics
- **Billing System**: Zuora, Stripe, Chargebee
- **Payment Gateway**: Stripe, PayPal, Square

### Secondary Data Sources
- **Bank Statements**: For cash receipt verification
- **Sales Contracts**: For terms and conditions
- **Customer Invoices**: For detailed transaction data
- **Purchase Orders**: For customer commitments

### Validation Procedures
- **Reconciliation**: Monthly reconciliation of all data sources
- **Sampling**: Random sampling of transactions for validation
- **Exception Reports**: Investigate and resolve exceptions
- **Management Review**: Management sign-off on reports

## Report Structure and Content

### Executive Summary
- **Key Metrics**: Highlight most important revenue metrics
- **Trends**: Summarize major trends and patterns
- **Outlook**: Provide forward-looking commentary
- **Action Items**: Identify required actions or follow-ups

### Revenue Breakdown
- **By Source**: Product vs. service revenue
- **By Geography**: Regional revenue performance
- **By Customer Segment**: Revenue by customer type
- **By Time Period**: Historical trend analysis

### Comparative Analysis
- **Period-over-Period**: Compare to previous periods
- **Budget vs. Actual**: Compare to budgeted amounts
- **Forecast vs. Actual**: Compare to forecasted amounts
- **Industry Benchmarks**: Compare to industry standards

## Quality Assurance

### Data Quality Checks
- **Completeness**: Verify all transactions are included
- **Accuracy**: Validate mathematical calculations
- **Consistency**: Check for consistent application of standards
- **Reasonableness**: Assess if figures are reasonable

### Review Process
- **Self-Review**: Initial review by preparer
- **Peer Review**: Review by colleague
- **Management Review**: Review by supervisor
- **Executive Review**: Review by senior management

### Error Correction
- **Identification**: Quickly identify errors when found
- **Correction**: Correct errors promptly
- **Disclosure**: Disclose material corrections
- **Prevention**: Implement controls to prevent recurrence
